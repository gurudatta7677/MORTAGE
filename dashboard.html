💡 Cross-Cutting Research Insights

🏛️ Institutional Maturity > Regulation Strictness

Singapore's deep market (46.9% M/GDP) with lowest LTV (75%) works because of public housing, CPF savings, mature credit bureaus. India's shallow market (11.1%) + informal 80% LTV due to weak infrastructure.

⏰ Timing of Regulation Matters

UAE introduced 2013 LTV/DBR caps *before* next boom—prevented 2008 crash repeat. NPL halved 6.1%→3.0%. Lesson: India needs DSTI caps now, not after next stress.

📊 HPI Volatility = Structural Fragility

UAE's 10.8% HPI std dev = exposed to oil shocks, expat flows. Singapore's 4.2% = policy-cushioned. India's 1.1% = stable, end-user driven. Volatility reveals systemic risk.

📈 Leverage Paths Converging but Challenges Differ

Singapore (111%) sustainable via buffers. UAE (25%, rising) needs proactive DBR recalibration. India (36%) faces invisible cumulative leverage = regulatory arbitrage risk.

🔧 Infrastructure Before Credit Expansion

Markets that build institutions first (Singapore) = self-reinforcing prudent depth. Markets that leapfrog (Dubai 2007) = agonizing corrections. India at critical juncture.

📌 One-Size-Fits-None Policy

Singapore needs to cool speculation. UAE needs to monitor leverage. India needs to prevent over-extension. Each market's challenge is different; recommendations tailored accordingly.
