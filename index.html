Research Overview

Question: How do different regulatory frameworks and market structures impact housing finance stability and growth across diverse economies?

Scope: Comparative analysis of Singapore, UAE, and India mortgage markets (2013–2024), examining market depth, regulatory effectiveness, asset quality, and household leverage.

Key Findings:

  • Singapore's 46.9% Mortgage-to-GDP reflects institutional maturity, not just regulation
  • UAE's rapid growth (7% → 16%) shows vulnerability to external shocks but improving stability
  • India faces critical regulatory gap: no formal DSTI cap despite 8%+ credit growth
  • Timing of regulation matters as much as content (UAE's proactive 2013 measures prevented 2008 repeat)
  • Housing price volatility is diagnostic of structural fragility (UAE 10.8% vs India 1.1%)

Impact: Policy makers, bankers, and researchers can use this evidence-based comparative framework to make informed decisions on macroprudential policy and housing finance regulation.
